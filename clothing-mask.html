Fitted Mask Design


Concept:

I was working long hours in the early days of the pandemic, and spent a lot of time in a mask every day. The surgical masks provided at the time had many flaws due to their one-size-fits-all design that I decided to take a shot at fixing. This design was drawn based on a contour of my face in profile for more comfortable and more functional fit. This design is the result of many successive iterations of improvements.

Materials

The side panels of the mask are made of twill, to give the shape some integrity. The center panel is made of polyester, and there are stretch mesh connecive peices on either side of the nose.

Assembly

The design is two layers. The inner layer is mesh, allowing air to pass freely out of your nose and get caught by the outer layer instead. This prevents the mask from trapping the humidity in your breath against your skin, which can get very uncomfortable over long periods of time. The outer layer is pleated, in order to double up the thickness of the material to help compensate for the loss of stopping power in the mesh inner layer. There is a slot in between the layers that an additional filter can be added.
