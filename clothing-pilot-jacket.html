Pilot Jacket


Concept:

This jacket was an attempt to address a common problem with biking in Chicago in early spring/late fall. The weather is always cool enough to require a jacket, but within minutes of getting in motion, you heat up and inevitably need to stop to take it off. By making the torso out of mesh panels, this jacket allows air resistance to cool off your torso while you're in motion, while still giving you some amount of insulation while at rest.

Materials

Shoulders and hip line are made with heavy twill fabric, so that the places where a backpack, and a bike lock slung over the shouler are durable. The torso panels are polyester breathable mesh, and the details on the collar and left arm are made of seatbelt webbing.

Assembly

This jacket features low front pockets, and lining. Everything is assembled with flat-felled seams for durability and to accomodate the stretch mesh fabric.
